# FoFit Landing Ultra Design Doctrine

## North Star

FoFit should read as a serious training operating system, not another fitness template or generic AI landing page.

The page thesis:

Most fitness apps track what happened. Generic AI apps generate workouts in isolation. FoFit adapts training around the real context of the week.

Primary emotional idea:

Train honestly. Cypher remembers the week your plan forgot.

## Product Positioning

FoFit is for lifters, athletes, and coaches.

Cypher is not a chatbot. Cypher is a training memory layer.

FoFit remembers:

- Training history
- Soreness
- Schedule pressure
- Equipment
- Nutrition
- Role
- Coach/team context

## Landing Page Jobs

1. Say what FoFit is in five seconds.
2. Say who it is for.
3. Make the core difference impossible to miss.
4. Show product logic, not just claims.
5. Build trust before asking for action.
6. Repeat the CTA without sounding desperate.
7. Make mobile feel first-class.
8. Load fast.
9. Use hierarchy so the visitor always knows where to look.
10. Avoid distracting animation.
11. Make every section answer a real objection.
12. Make the design feel like one system.
13. Use proof, product mechanics, transcripts, and surfaces.
14. Avoid generic AI wording.
15. Make the product feel hard to copy.

## Visual System

The page should feel like:

- Linear-level software clarity
- Oura-level wellness polish
- Tracksmith-level athletic taste
- Vercel-level interaction detail
- Apple-level restraint
- FoFit's dark editorial identity

Design constraints:

- Dark editorial base
- Sharp typography
- Blue/cyan accents used with restraint
- Green only when describing useful Cypher/product state
- Few large visual ideas instead of many template cards
- Product surfaces should look intentional and code-native
- Section rhythm should vary density without changing brand language

## Copy Rules

Use concrete product language:

- Memory
- Training context
- Real week
- Schedule pressure
- Soreness
- Equipment
- Fuel
- Coach/team context
- Approval
- Adjustment
- Training block

Avoid:

- "AI fitness companion"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Transform your journey"
- Empty hype
- Fake claims
- Unsupported institutional proof

## Section Doctrine

1. Hero: make the thesis unavoidable.
2. Context Memory: answer "what does it remember?"
3. Three Paths: answer "is this for me?"
4. Cypher Transcript: answer "is this just a chatbot?"
5. Daily Loop: answer "how does it work in a real day?"
6. Product Surfaces: answer "is there a real product?"
7. Fuel System: answer "does nutrition connect to training?"
8. FoFit Score: answer "how do I see progress?"
9. Teams: answer "can coaches use this?"
10. Pricing: answer "what is the early offer?"
11. Final CTA: close on the emotional thesis.

## Product Visual Doctrine

The first viewport may keep background video, but it must not depend on video to feel premium.

Hero should include a CSS product cockpit with:

- Phone surface
- Readiness/adaptation state
- Active session
- Cypher message
- Floating memory cards

Visuals must show system logic:

- Context enters memory.
- Memory changes today's plan.
- Today's session generates tomorrow's context.

## Mobile Doctrine

Mobile should not be a compressed desktop page.

Rules:

- Hero copy should stay readable.
- Product cockpit should collapse cleanly.
- CTAs should remain obvious.
- No horizontal scroll.
- No tiny unreadable mono labels.
- Cards should become clean stacked surfaces.
- Motion should reduce gracefully.

## CTA Doctrine

Use waitlist CTAs at natural decision points:

- Hero
- Role cards
- Pricing
- Final CTA

CTA language should be confident:

- "Join the founding waitlist"
- "Join as a lifter"
- "Join as an athlete"
- "Join as a coach"

Do not overuse urgency. Founding pricing is proof, not pressure.
